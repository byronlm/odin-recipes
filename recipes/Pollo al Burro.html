Pollo al Burro

Description

Pollo al Burro is a classic Italian dish from the region of Emilia-Romagna, especially famous in Florence and Bologna. It features tender chicken, usually breast or thighs, sautéed in butter and often finished with sage, lemon juice, or white wine. The butter creates a rich, velvety sauce that coats the chicken, making it simple yet deeply flavorful. It's typically served with crusty bread or light vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 100 g (about 7 tbsp) unsalted butter
  • 2–3 fresh sage leaves (optional)
  • 1 clove garlic (lightly crushed)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of ½ lemon (optional)
  • ¼ cup dry white wine (optional)

Steps

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Add the garlic and sage leaves (if using) and sauté for 1–2 minutes to infuse the butter.
  4. Add the chicken and cook for about 5–7 minutes per side, or until golden and fully cooked through.
  5. Optional: Deglaze the pan with white wine and let it simmer for 2–3 minutes.
  6. Optional: Squeeze lemon juice over the chicken for extra brightness.
  7. Remove from heat and serve with the buttery sauce spooned over the top.
